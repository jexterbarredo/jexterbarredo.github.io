Galaxies

Galaxies are vast systems made up of stars, planets, gas, dust, and dark matter, all held together by gravity. Each galaxy can contain millions to trillions of stars, and they come in various shapes such as spiral, elliptical, and irregular. Our own galaxy, the Milky Way, is a spiral galaxy that houses our solar system and billions of other stars. Galaxies play a fundamental role in the structure and evolution of the universe. They serve as the building blocks of the cosmos, where stars are born, evolve, and eventually die. Through the life cycles of stars, galaxies help distribute essential elements like carbon and oxygen—key ingredients for life—across space. Studying galaxies also helps scientists understand the history and expansion of the universe. Observing distant galaxies allows astronomers to look back in time and uncover how the universe has changed over billions of years. Galaxies are not just collections of stars—they are crucial to unlocking the mysteries of space, time, and the origins of everything around us.
