Blackholes

A black hole is a mysterious object in space with an incredibly strong pull of gravity. It forms when a massive star collapses in on itself, creating a point so dense that even light cannot escape. Because of this, black holes appear completely dark and are invisible to the naked eye. Scientists detect them by observing how nearby stars and light behave around them. Black holes are important because they help us learn more about how the universe works. They play a key role in the life and structure of galaxies, often sitting at their centers. Studying black holes also gives scientists clues about gravity, space, and time—especially in extreme conditions that we can't recreate on Earth. .
